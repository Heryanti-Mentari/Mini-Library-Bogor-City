Argumentative smart surveilance in Bogor city

Argumentative Text Description

Topik : Smart Surveillance in Bogor City
Judul : Will the implementation of CCTV and facial recognition in Bogor City improve security, or will it threaten residents' privacy?

introduction

In recent years, the smart city concept has developed rapidly in Indonesia, including in Bogor. One crucial element in smart city development is the use of smart surveillance systems, including the installation of intelligent CCTV, data integration with command centers, and the potential use of facial recognition technology. This technology is believed to improve city security by providing real-time monitoring capabilities. However, concerns have arisen that digital surveillance systems could threaten citizens' privacy, particularly when data management is not transparent.

Therefore, this essay argues that although CCTV and facial recognition can improve security in Bogor City, their implementation must still be limited by strict regulations to prevent privacy violations and data misuse.

Argument 1: CCTV can improve security and quick response

One of the primary reasons for using CCTV in public spaces is to enhance security. The Bogor City Government has installed dozens of cameras at various strategic locations, such as intersections, City Hall, public spaces, and city parks. All of these cameras are integrated into the Bogor Green Room, a command center that monitors city conditions in real time.

This type of surveillance has been proven to assist authorities in handling crime, accidents, and emergencies. For example, the Bogor Police Department once stated that city CCTV cameras helped speed up the identification of criminals in densely populated areas. From a cause and effect perspective, the more areas monitored, the less likely a crime is to occur because perpetrators feel they are being watched.

Furthermore, CCTV plays a crucial role in traffic management. Camera footage helps traffic control officers regulate traffic lights and detect congestion. Thus, CCTV truly serves as a crime prevention tool while supporting urban mobility control.

Argument 2: Facial recognition has the potential to increase the effectiveness of law enforcement.

Cities around the world, such as Singapore and Seoul, have used facial recognition technology in public surveillance to speed up the search for fugitives or locate missing persons. If this technology were implemented in Bogor, the effectiveness of law enforcement could be increased.

From a utility perspective, facial recognition can identify individuals in seconds—much faster than manual methods. This can assist police in handling serious cases such as kidnapping, terrorism, or street violence.

Even in the context of disasters or large crowds, this technology can support more accurate victim searches. The causal chain is clear: the faster identification is made, the faster rescue or law enforcement action can be initiated. From an urban development perspective, the use of facial recognition could make Bogor one of the cities with a futuristic security system on par with developed cities in Asia.

Counterargument: Privacy threats & data misuse risks

However, the use of CCTV and facial recognition in public spaces also carries significant risks that should not be ignored. First, the lack of strong personal data protection regulations at the city level can lead to data misuse. Recordings of citizens' faces can be misused by certain parties, whether for political or commercial gain, or for targeting individuals without consent.

One clear example of data security issues is a 2024 report that stated that approximately 60 CCTV cameras in Bogor were damaged. If even the physical devices aren't properly managed, how can you ensure the security of sensitive data like facial recognition recordings?

Furthermore, many citizens may be unaware that their activities are being monitored, leading to feelings of discomfort and a violation of individual freedoms. Digital rights groups frequently warn that facial recognition has the potential to be used to profile citizens or monitor their political activities. This is a compelling reason why smart surveillance should not be implemented without strict regulations and oversight.

Rebuttal & Conclusion

While there are strong objections, this doesn't mean smart surveillance shouldn't be implemented. The solution isn't to stop the technology, but to create clear privacy protection regulations, for example:

  • 1. a maximum data retention rule of 30 days,
  • 2. regular security audits,
  • 3. transparency of camera locations,
  • 4. prohibition on the use of recordings for political or commercial purposes,
  • 5. public consent before using facial recognition.

With such regulations, surveillance technology can provide benefits without sacrificing citizens' rights.

In conclusion, CCTV and facial recognition have the potential to improve Bogor City's security, but they must be implemented with strict regulations to avoid threatening privacy. Bogor can become a safe, smart city that respects its citizens' digital rights if it can balance innovation and ethics.
